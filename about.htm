If you want the labels I take for myself: I am a furry, trans(gender/sexual), queer, pansexual, engineer, woman. :3 (+/- grammer)


While being a furry is not a gender or identity I include it as a part of who I am. Being a furry is a kind of creative outlet, a shared fascination in anthropomorphized animals, and a punk counterculture that's radically supportive, and vulnerable. Every single furry would probably give a different account of what it means to them. It's not just pretending to be an anthropomorphized animal. It's a freeing experience. There's a lot of song and dance that humans do to avoid coming off as weird or soft to other huamns. Since anthropomorphized animals don't exist, they can act however they want. Being a made up bat creature leaves behind shames and baggage that comes with being a person and allows me to express myself in ways that are weird or soft. I can be vulnerable and make art and I don't have to worry about being seen as weird or soft.

reading recommendations

(books and papers, in no particular order)
Whipping GirlJulia Serano
While not a perfect book, it really helped me better understand queer history I hadn't been exposed to as well as connected a lot of thoughts I had independently, untangling my thoughts and filling in gaps. Her ability to so clearly articulate what exactly transphobia is and why its based in misogyny was illuminating. I feel like this was able to fill gaps left in my upbringing.
Better off Without 'emChuck Tompson
A cynical and occassionaly funny analysis of the devide between north and south that persists in modern day. Thompson attempts to advicate for a peaceful secession as the best course of action for both the north and the south. His argument felt very tangible from my experiences as a southerner leftist. The south holds the rest of the country hostage, and it presents a naked view of the south.
Neoliberalism and the CityDavid Harvey
Hey were you also raised in the US public education system? Were you also stewed in neoliberal capitalist propaganda? Have a gander at a Marxist's summary of the last hundred or so years of economics and policy. It's a short paper, but if you've ever wondered why shit is so fucked this is why.
